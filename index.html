About Me

Dr. Praveen Meduri is an Assistant Professor in the School of Chemical Engineering at Oklahoma State University. His academic journey includes a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering from the University of Louisville (2010), an M.S. in Chemical Engineering from the University of Akron (2005), and a B.E. (Hons.) in Chemical Engineering from Birla Institute of Technology and Science (2003). He further honed his expertise through postdoctoral fellowships at Pennsylvania State University (2013-2014) and Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (2011-2013).

Prior to joining OSU, Dr. Meduri served as an Associate Professor and Assistant Professor of Chemical Engineering at IIT Hyderabad (2014-2023) and as a Research Engineer at Advanced Energy Materials LLC (2023-2024). His research group is dedicated to exploring the critical food-water-energy nexus, developing effective management strategies crucial for creating inclusive and sustainable solutions to global challenges.

His work involves deep investigation into advanced functional materials and electrochemical heterogeneous catalysis. He utilizes state-of-the-art synthesis and characterization techniques to study complex systems, employing combinatorial approaches to understand material interactions within catalytic processes. Dr. Meduri's specific broad areas of interest include:

  • **Photocatalysis & Electrocatalysis:** Focusing on water splitting for hydrogen production and hydrogen peroxide production.
  • **Energy Storage:** Researching metal-ion batteries, lithium-sulfur batteries, and metal-air batteries with in-situ mechanistic studies.
  • **Water Purification:** Developing electrochemical approaches for the complete mineralization of per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS).
  • **Carbon Dioxide Conversion:** Exploring methods for converting CO2 into value-added fuels.
  • **Catalysis for Agriculture:** Dedicated to developing selective and efficient catalysts for nitrogen reduction reaction (NRR).

Selected Publications & Patents

Dr. Meduri has published more than 30 refereed journal and conference papers and holds three U.S. patents. Below are some of his recent and notable contributions.

Property tunable two-dimensional zinc sulfoselenides as photocatalysts for enhanced hydrogen peroxide production: Experimental and DFT analyses

*Journal of Catalysis*, 2024. This research explores novel photocatalysts for efficient hydrogen peroxide production.

**Full Abstract:** This study delves into the synthesis and characterization of two-dimensional zinc sulfoselenides, investigating their potential as highly efficient photocatalysts for hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) production. Through a combination of experimental techniques and Density Functional Theory (DFT) analyses, we elucidate the structure-property relationships that govern their photocatalytic activity. The research highlights how tuning the composition of these materials can significantly enhance their performance, offering a promising pathway for sustainable and green chemical synthesis.

Exotic femtosecond nonlinear optical properties of laser ablated MoS2 quantum dots

*Optical Materials*, 2024. Investigates the unique optical properties of MoS2 quantum dots for advanced applications.

**Full Abstract:** This work focuses on the fascinating femtosecond nonlinear optical properties of molybdenum disulfide (MoS2) quantum dots synthesized via laser ablation. We explore their strong light-matter interactions at ultrafast timescales, revealing unique characteristics that make them highly attractive for applications in photonics, optical switching, and advanced sensing. The study provides critical insights into the fundamental physics governing these exotic optical phenomena.

Mechanistic insights into enhanced photocatalytic H2O2 production induced by a Z-scheme heterojunction of copper bismuth oxide and molybdenum sulfide

*Journal of Materials Chemistry A*, 2023. Focuses on improving hydrogen peroxide production through specific material combinations.

**Full Abstract:** This research provides detailed mechanistic insights into the enhanced photocatalytic production of hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) using a novel Z-scheme heterojunction composed of copper bismuth oxide (CuBi2O4) and molybdenum sulfide (MoS2). We employ advanced spectroscopic and electrochemical techniques to unravel the charge transfer pathways and synergistic effects within this composite system, demonstrating a significant improvement in H2O2 yield and selectivity under visible light irradiation.

Doped micro-silicon and vanadium carbide MXene composite as anode for high stability and high capacity Li-ion batteries

*Results in Engineering*, 2023. Research on advanced anode materials for high-performance lithium-ion batteries.

**Full Abstract:** This study presents the development of a high-performance anode material for lithium-ion batteries, utilizing a composite of doped micro-silicon and vanadium carbide MXene. We demonstrate that this novel composite exhibits exceptional cycling stability and high charge/discharge capacity, addressing key limitations of conventional anode materials. The research provides a pathway for next-generation energy storage devices with improved longevity and efficiency.

U.S. Patent: Novel Materials for Energy Storage

One of three U.S. patents held, focusing on innovative materials for next-generation energy storage solutions.

**Full Abstract:** This U.S. Patent describes novel material compositions and methods for their synthesis, specifically designed for advanced energy storage applications. The patented technology focuses on enhancing the performance, safety, and lifespan of various battery systems, including but not limited to lithium-ion and solid-state batteries. This innovation represents a significant step towards more efficient and sustainable energy solutions.
